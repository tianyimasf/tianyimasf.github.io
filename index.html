I'm a Graduate Assistant at University of Colorado Boulder. In my free time I build new data science projects, study game dev and architectural modeling.

My research at CU Boulder focuses on gender and racial biases in Chinese and English Generative AI models. I generated 18,000 facial images using Chinese and English prompts and 4 different stable diffusion models.

Before I became a research assistant, I was a software engineer at the Bay Area for a year, working on pricing prediction for my company's marketplace.

I have been in the research field since freshmen summer in college. Prof. Lenhart Schubert was kind enough to let me help with his PhD students' research on Natural Language Processing. Later, I participated in my great math advisor, Prof. Alex Iosevich's summer program on theoretical and computational combinatorics, optimization and data science.

I love coding and analyzing all kinds of data! (At least when the code doesn't have bugs.) Crafting a story from unexplored data is my passion, and I love learning from others thoughts and experiences too! Feel free to connect with me on LinkedIn or shoot a message/email.
